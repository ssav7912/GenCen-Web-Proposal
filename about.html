About Us

The Gender Centre is a NSW based residency support, counselling and advocacy organisation for Transgender and Gender-diverse people across all ages and backgrounds. We are a non-profit organisation registered with the Australian Charities and Not-for-Profits Commission. We are funded in large part by the NSW government through the Department of Human Services.

We offer a wide range of services for Transgender and Gender-diverse people in the Sydney metro and regional NSW area. These include:

  1. Residency Support for homeless or distressed people
  2. Individual case management
  3. Specialist counseling services with professional psychologists
  4. Reference programs for psychiatrists, endocrinologists and other specialists
  5. Advocacy and legal resources
  6. Outreach and community programs
  7. Sexual Health and safety programs and information

Our History

We began as the ATA - an advocacy and support network for transgender women in Sydney's Kings Cross in the 1980s. These women faced significant problems with abuse and homelessness and so "Tiresias House" was created in 1983 in Petersham to provide a homeless shelter. This was funded by the Department of Main Roads.

Over the next decade several more houses would be added to this network, in Suburbs like Ashfield and Haberfield. In 1984, a seperate halfway house was created for gaol leavers.

During the AIDs crisis throughout the 1980s, this network of residency support houses partnered with NSW health to help promote sexual health and safety in the LGBTQI+ community.

In 1991 this network was incorporated into the Gender Centre, also introducing counseling services, advocacy and legal support, and many other services.
